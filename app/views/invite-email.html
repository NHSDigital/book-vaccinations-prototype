Subject: Booking your NHS respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) vaccine

Dear ((fullName)),

You can book a free NHS RSV vaccine because of your age.

RSV is a virus that affects the airways and lungs and can make it difficult to breathe. Your age group has a higher risk of getting seriously ill from RSV infection.

How to get your RSV vaccine

You can get your RSV vaccine in one of the following ways:

  • Book a pharmacy appointment at www.nhs.uk/book-rsv
  • Find a pharmacy walk-in site at www.nhs.uk/rsv-walk-in
  • Contact your GP surgery

If your GP surgery cannot book you in now, please ask when they will have more RSV appointments.

If you are aged 80 years, your last chance to get the RSV vaccine is 31 August 2025.

Why the NHS is offering the RSV vaccine

RSV infections occur all year round and can be hard to avoid. Every year, thousands of older adults need hospital care for RSV, and some of them die. RSV infection can cause shortness of breath and lead to pneumonia and other life-threatening conditions.

Having the RSV vaccine reduces your risk of severe symptoms. Just 1 dose gives most people good protection for a few years.

More information

Other NHS vaccines for people aged under 80

If you haven't already, you can also get free NHS pneumococcal and shingles vaccines. Contact your GP surgery if you missed either vaccine. For details, go to www.nhs.uk/vaccinations.
