Is {{data['first-name']}} {{data['last-name']}} able to get a free flu vaccine?

As they're under 65, they're only able to get a free flu vaccine if they:

  • have a serious long-term health condition
  • are having treatment or medication which means they are at increased risk
  • are pregnant
  • are in long-term residential care
  • receive a carer's allowance, or those who are the main carer of an elderly or disabled person who may be at risk if they get sick
  • live with or are in close contact with someone more likely to get infections (such as someone who has HIV, has had a transplant, is having certain treatments for cancer, lupus or rheumatoid arthritis)
  • a frontline health or social care worker (if their workplace doesn't offer an occupational health scheme)

They might be asked some questions at the pharmacy to check they're eligible for a free flu vaccine.
